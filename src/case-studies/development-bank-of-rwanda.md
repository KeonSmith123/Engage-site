context: |-
  This East Africa-based development bank employs approximately 200 people. The relationship began with a job evaluation and grading project aimed at creating a more effective organisational framework to support employee growth, reward management, and broader talent practices.
challenge: |-
  The bank's existing grading approach did not provide sufficient room for career progression, particularly at lower levels where roles were grouped into overly broad bands. This limited the organisation's ability to differentiate roles, create visible progression pathways, and manage employee growth in a structured way.
approach: |-
  We worked with the client in-country to facilitate a job evaluation process supported by a trained local committee, with contributions from subject matter experts and engagement from senior leadership, including the CEO. A new grading structure was designed with additional layers at the lower end, creating greater differentiation and clearer career progression. We then undertook a compensation analysis to align reward management with external market realities and the new internal structure.
outcome: |-
  The bank now has a more differentiated grading structure with greater space for career progression at lower levels. The engagement later expanded to include the design of a competency framework, reflecting both the strategic value of the partnership and the client's trust in the approach.
