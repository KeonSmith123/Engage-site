context: |-
  This large financial services organisation operates across Kenya and Uganda and employs approximately 800 people. The client engaged us to support a broad job evaluation exercise as part of a wider effort to strengthen grading, improve internal consistency, and align reward structures across the organisation.
challenge: |-
  The organisation needed to move from its existing grading structure to a new one that better reflected role size and organisational realities. The project also needed to address grade conversion, compensation alignment, and job title consistency, plus build buy-in at multiple levels, including senior leadership, and support implementation and change management.
approach: |-
  We worked in-country over two weeks, training a local committee of leaders and employees to systematically grade around 200 positions. Early in the project the CEO requested an awareness session for senior leaders to build buy-in. An executive-level committee was then constituted to review outcomes through several iterations before final CEO sign-off. The engagement later expanded into implementation support, including individual employee letters and an organisation-wide communication campaign.
outcome: |-
  A successfully implemented grading structure, improved compensation alignment, stronger job title consistency, leadership buy-in across the organisation, and a structured communication and change process that ensured the outcomes were implemented effectively.
